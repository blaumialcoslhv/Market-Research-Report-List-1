<p><p>WLAN security market encompasses various encryption protocols to safeguard wireless networks. WEP (Wired Equivalent Privacy) is the oldest and least secure, vulnerable to attacks. WPA (Wi-Fi Protected Access) improved security but still has flaws. WPA2, the most widely adopted, uses stronger encryption, ensuring better protection. Despite these advancements, some networks operate without encryption, exposing them to risks. Each market type reflects a balance between security needs and technological advancements, influencing users' choices based on their requirements.</p></p>
